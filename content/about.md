# About Me

Hi, I’m Halil, also known online as OblivionSage.

I work on offensive security with a focus on low-level vulnerabilities, memory-safety issues, and protocol flaws.
Most of my time goes into reverse engineering, fuzzing, or reading code that was never meant to be read by humans in the first place.

I enjoy breaking assumptions in real systems , not theoretical papers or toy examples.
Bugs that slip through code reviews, hidden integer overflows, unsafe deserialization paths, weird edge cases in network protocols… that’s usually where I spend my time.

Over the past years I’ve reported vulnerabilities across different open-source projects.
Some of them have been published as CVEs, including issues in cryptographic libraries, drivers, and high-traffic platforms.
I mostly use a mix of fuzzers (libFuzzer, honggfuzz), sanitizers (ASan/UBSan), and manual Reverse Engineering work to dig into code until it breaks.

Outside the technical side, I try to document what I learn - either through public reports, write-ups, or contributions to open-source projects.
This blog is simply a place to archive that work.

If you’re into reverse engineering, exploit development, or security research in general, feel free to reach out.
